You found the lab note. The creamy sound is the agent's recreation of a Rainy 75, made by dissecting a recording of the real board strike by strike: each keystroke is a short bright contact click at 1.4 to 2.8kHz that dies within ~10ms, a quiet 60-110Hz thump that blooms a few milliseconds later and rings ~40ms at roughly a quarter of the strike's peak (I measured 23% in the recording and calibrated to exactly that), a second lighter click 35 to 80ms later which is the key release, and a ~4x loudness spread between grazed and bottomed-out keys. Synthesized live in WebAudio. No samples were harmed.

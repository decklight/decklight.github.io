Paper Planes

Fold your first dart — then watch it fly

One sheet of paper, six creases, one flight. Press the right arrow to begin.

What you need

  • One sheet of A4 or Letter — ordinary printer paper flies best
  • A flat table — every crease should be pressed sharp
  • Two minutes
Almost nothing. ⟨CLICK⟩ Ordinary printer paper is the right weight — heavier stock stalls. ⟨CLICK⟩ Sharp creases are the whole game, so fold on a table and press each one flat. ⟨CLICK⟩ That's it. Two minutes.

Fold the dart

Six creases, in order

Six creases, and order matters. ⟨CLICK⟩ First, crease down the middle and unfold — every later fold references this line. ⟨CLICK⟩ Fold both top corners in so they meet at the crease. ⟨CLICK⟩ Then fold the new slanted edges to the middle again — the nose gets sharp. ⟨CLICK⟩ Fold the whole thing in half away from you, so the flaps end up outside. ⟨CLICK⟩ Fold each wing down level with the body. ⟨CLICK⟩ Flare the wings out flat, and it's ready.

Watch it fly

Six creases later

And this is why you folded it. Throw level and smooth, not hard — a dart wants glide, not force.
